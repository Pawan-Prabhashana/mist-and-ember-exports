**Last updated:** October 2025

## 🌿 Brand Voice Overview

Ceylon Roots’ tone is **authentic**, **elegant**, and **trustworthy** — rooted in Sri Lanka’s heritage, yet modern and global in expression.

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | Description | Example |
|------------|--------------|----------|
| **Authentic** | Grounded in real Sri Lankan tea culture, tradition, and expertise. | “Sourced from the misty hills of Nuwara Eliya, every leaf tells the story of Ceylon.” |
| **Elegant** | Sophisticated but never pretentious — refined choice of words, clean tone. | “An exquisite balance of aroma, color, and taste.” |
| **Trustworthy** | Confident and transparent — built on quality and certification. | “Certified under Lion Logo, ISO 22000, HACCP, and GMP standards.” |
| **Warm & Welcoming** | Friendly, inclusive, and human — speak with care and pride. | “We invite you to share a cup that connects cultures.” |
| **Global & Professional** | Clear English with universal tone for international audiences. | “Partnering with tea enthusiasts and retailers across 40 countries.” |

## ✍️ Core Writing Principles

1. **Write for people first, search engines second.**
   - Prioritize clarity and readability.
   - Avoid jargon or technical terms unless necessary.

2. **Keep it concise and natural.**
   - Sentences under 20 words are ideal.
   - Each paragraph expresses one clear idea.

3. **Use consistent terminology.**
   - Always say “Ceylon Roots” (never “the company” or “CR”).
   - Use “Pure Ceylon Tea” for brand consistency.
   - Refer to “Sri Lanka” (not “Ceylon”) except in historical or certification contexts.

4. **Be globally understandable.**
   - Use simple English that’s easy to translate.
   - Avoid region-specific idioms or slang.

5. **Celebrate Sri Lankan heritage subtly.**
   - Highlight craftsmanship, nature, and culture without clichés.
   - Example: “Inspired by the lush tea gardens of Sri Lanka” — not “from the land of elephants and spice.”

## 🧭 Structure & Formatting Rules

### Headings
- Use sentence case (only capitalize the first word and proper nouns).
  - ✅ “Our story”  
  - ❌ “OUR STORY” or “Our Story”

### Paragraphs
- Limit to 3–4 sentences each.
- Leave space between paragraphs for readability.

### Lists
- Use bullet points for clarity.
- Start each bullet with a capital letter and no ending punctuation.

### Numbers
- Write numbers **1–9** as words; **10+** as numerals.  
  Example: “three varieties”, “25 years of experience”

### Units & Measurements
- Use metric system (g, kg, ml) consistently.
- Include space between number and unit: `250 g`, `1.5 L`

### Dates
- Format as: `20 October 2025`
- Never abbreviate months (avoid “Oct.” or “10/20/25”).

## 🧾 Grammar & Punctuation

| Rule | Example |
|------|----------|
| Use Oxford comma | “Black, green, and herbal teas.” |
| Use “&” only in headings or logos | “Research & Development” |
| Use en-dash (–) for ranges | “2022–2025” |
| Avoid exclamation marks in product descriptions | Maintain elegant tone |
| Use contractions sparingly | “We’re proud” is fine; avoid overuse |

## 💬 Tone by Context

### Website Content
- Balance **informative** and **emotional**.
- Showcase expertise and story equally.
- Example:  
  > “From Sri Lanka’s central highlands to your cup, every leaf from Ceylon Roots carries the legacy of centuries-old craftsmanship.”

### Product Descriptions
- Focus on **sensory language**: aroma, color, texture, and feeling.
- Keep each under 60 words.
- Example:  
  > “A golden liquor with a smooth, honeyed aroma — our Premium Ceylon Black Tea is handpicked from the misty hills of Nuwara Eliya.”

### Corporate / B2B Communication
- Formal and factual, highlighting certifications and production quality.
- Example:  
  > “Ceylon Roots is a Lion Logo, ISO 22000, HACCP, and GMP certified tea exporter serving over 40 countries worldwide.”

### Social Media
- Warm, personal, and community-driven.
- Use active voice and short sentences.
- Example:  
  > “Every cup tells a story — brewed in Sri Lanka, shared across the world.”  
  > #CeylonRoots #PureCeylonTea #SriLankanHeritage

### Sustainability & CSR
- Be transparent and humble.  
- Avoid “greenwashing”; use measurable outcomes.
- Example:  
  > “We reduce waste by using biodegradable packaging and support local reforestation projects across Uva and Kandy.”

## 🧩 Component Copy Guidelines

| Section | Character Limit | Voice & Purpose |
|----------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Hero Headline** | 60 | Short, evocative, and brand-driven |
| **Subheadline** | 120 | Clarifies value or product uniqueness |
| **Button / CTA** | 25 | Action-oriented, clear (“Discover More”, “Start Your Brand”) |
| **Meta Descriptions** | 160 | SEO-friendly, plain English summary |
| **Testimonials** | 100 | Authentic, conversational tone |
| **Error Messages** | 60 | Polite, helpful, human (“Please check your email address.”) |

## 🌍 Localization & Multilingual Support

- Default language: **English (en)**  
- Support for **Sinhala (si)** and **Tamil (ta)** planned for regional audience expansion.  
- Maintain cultural neutrality in English content.  
- Localize measurements, packaging details, and tea terms as needed.  

---

## 📷 Visual & Caption Guidelines

- Every image must include descriptive alt text.  
  Example: `alt="Ceylon Roots team handpicking tea leaves in the highlands of Sri Lanka."`

- Captions should add context, not repeat alt text.  
  Example: “Handpicked in the morning mist — our signature Ceylon harvest.”

- Use title case for captions, sentence case for alt text.

---

## 🧠 SEO & Metadata Writing Rules

- Use **one H1 per page** (clear, keyword-relevant).  
- Meta title ≤ 60 characters.  
- Meta description ≤ 160 characters.  
- Avoid keyword stuffing — write naturally.  
- Always include brand name: “Ceylon Roots | Pure Ceylon Tea Exporter”.

---

## 🤝 Inclusivity & Ethical Language

- Use respectful, inclusive language — avoid gendered terms or stereotypes.  
- Say “tea pluckers” or “tea experts”, not “tea ladies” or “tea boys”.  
- Emphasize empowerment and collaboration, not charity.  
- Example:  
  > “We work with smallholder farmers to build sustainable livelihoods and preserve Sri Lanka’s tea heritage.”
