# Our Story

We started this publication with a simple idea: **stories can change decisions**.  
From people building resilient communities to founders shaping new markets, we cover the ideas that turn into impact.

## What We Believe

- **Clarity over noise.** Explain the moving parts, name the trade-offs, show the data.  
- **Local voices first.** Lived experience is a source, not a footnote.  
- **Accountability.** Credit where due, corrections when needed.

---

## How We Work

**Field Notes → Verification → Narrative.**  
We combine ground reporting, primary documents, and expert review to produce concise, well-sourced pieces.

- **Field notes:** on-the-ground observations, interviews, and photo logs.  
- **Verification:** triangulate with datasets, public filings, and domain experts.  
- **Narrative:** connect facts to the decisions readers need to make.

> We keep our editorial and commercial decisions separate. Branded content is clearly labeled.

---

## Timeline

- **2019 — Drafting the blueprint.** A small newsletter with long-form explainers.  
- **2021 — First partnerships.** Collaborations with local correspondents and co-ops.  
- **2023 — Product revamp.** Accessible design, faster load, mobile-first typography.  
- **2025 — Today.** An open library of explainers, field diaries, and data-backed briefs.

---

## Sections We Publish

- **News & Briefs:** fast context for fast decisions.  
- **Field Diaries:** photo-rich dispatches from the ground.  
- **Explainers:** neutral, sourced primers on complex topics.  
- **Playbooks:** practical how-to guides from practitioners.

---

## Editorial Standards

- **Sourcing:** at least two independent confirmations for contested claims.  
- **Attribution:** we cite contributors and link to original materials where possible.  
- **Corrections:** if we get something wrong, we fix it and note the change history.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do you accept op-eds?**  
Yes, if they disclose material interests and add unique expertise.

**Can I reuse your graphics?**  
Most charts are under a permissive license; credit and link back. Check each post’s footer for terms.

**How are features funded?**  
Editorial budget comes from subscriptions and grants. Sponsored work is labeled **Sponsored** and follows separate guidelines.
